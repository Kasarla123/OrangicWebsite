Bringing Awareness on Organic Farming

Choose organic for the health of your family and the planet. Organic means more nutrition, fewer chemicals, mitigated climate change, and healthier ecosystems.

Healthy Soil

Organic farming starts with healthy soil. Farmers put nutrients back into the earth using sustainable methods like crop rotation, cover crops, hand weeding, mulch, manure and compost. Healthy soil grows more nutritious food and is able to store carbon dioxide helping to combat climate change.

Healthy Environment

Organic farming combats climate change by storing carbon dioxide in healthy soil and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.fossil fuel-based fertilizers and most synthetic pesticides are prohibited. Organic farmers are prohibited from using toxic chemicals that pollute the environment.

Humane Animal Treatment

Organic farmers accommodate the natural behavior of their livestock. They provide them with safe, cage-free living conditions and access to the outdoors so they can roam and graze. Animals must be free to do what comes naturally. so that, livestock are getting the right nutrition.

No Synthetic Nitrogen Fertilizers

Chemical fertilizer used in conventional agriculture is one of the largest polluters of our drinking water today. Organic farmers do not use synthetic fertilisers, increasing soil fertility through strategies such as working crop wastes and manure into the soil.

No Toxic Persistent Pesticides

Organic farmers are prohibited from using toxic persistent pesticides that pollute the environment, harm pollinators, and contaminant your food.

Minimal Processing

Organic foods go through minimal processing. No artificial colors, flavors or preservatives are allowed. You won't see any Red #40 or Blue Lake or Yellow #5 in the ingredients lists of any organic foods.
